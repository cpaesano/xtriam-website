# Introduction to bpmPro

Welcome to bpmPro Contractor, the premier tailored software for windows and doors contractors. This introductory video walks you through the main data structure to help you begin using the software, designed to make your operations efficient and elevate the customer experience.

With bpmPro, you can access your critical data from multiple devices, including desktops, tablets, and cell phones. This software is uniquely designed for window dealers like you, helping you grow through every phase of the customer journey.

---

## The Four Phases of a Window Project

### 1. Engaging the Client

This phase includes handling sales and marketing activities, creating proposals and contracts, scheduling sales presentations, and maintaining communication with clients.

### 2. Managing the Order

After closing a deal, you'll need to gather all the necessary information to put together the order and release it for production. This includes coordinating final measurements, obtaining building permits, and entering all required purchase orders.

### 3. Delivery

In the delivery phase, you receive the order from the factory, communicate tentative installation dates to clients, schedule installations, manage warehouse inventory, report shortages, handle warranty issues, and document the installation process with before, during, and after photos.

### 4. Post-Sales

The system supports you during the post-sales phase by documenting potential warranty claims and communicating with clients if support cases arise.

## What bpmPro Covers

bpmPro navigates through all aspects of these phases:

- **Prospecting** — Finding and qualifying leads
- **Estimating** — Building accurate quotes
- **Negotiating** — Managing proposals and contracts
- **Final Measurement Coordination** — Scheduling and tracking measurements
- **Product Management** — Tracking orders and inventory
- **Building Permit Handling** — Managing the permit process
- **Warehouse Management** — Receiving and organizing products
- **Installation Scheduling** — Coordinating install dates and crews
- **Job Supervision** — Overseeing active installations
- **Customer Satisfaction Follow-Up** — Post-install check-ins
- **Warranty Management** — Tracking claims and resolutions
- **Service Support** — Handling ongoing service requests

Throughout these processes, the system facilitates communication with customers, sends invoices, collects payments, calculates real-time sales commissions, and tracks key performance indicators like sales revenue and lead conversions.

## The Data Structure

A crucial aspect of any system is its data structure. bpmPro boasts a robust data structure that, once familiarized, reveals its powerful capabilities in managing your data.

### Account

Everything begins at the **Account** level. An account can be a homeowner, contractor, architect firm, engineering firm, or vendor. This is the main module where you start if the contact does not already exist in the system.

### Contact

Once you create an account, you'll create a **Contact** — which could be a homeowner, a company employee, a contractor employee, or a vendor employee.

### Project

After establishing the account and contact, you move on to create a **Project** (or multiple projects) for that account. A project does not necessarily mean you are already executing it — it's the opportunity to quote and hopefully win the project.

### Sales Document

Under each project, you create **Sales Documents**. It's common to have one or more sales documents per project. For example, you might have a standard version versus a Kynar paint proposal.

### Line Items

Below each sales document, you create **Product and Labor Items**. For items that don't fit the typical window or door product, you can create **Specialty Items** — such as special demolition work, unique installation requirements, opening modifications, or the rental of special equipment.

---

## Summary

The main data structure flows as:

**Account** → **Contact** → **Project** → **Sales Document** → **Product & Labor Items**

Throughout the process, you can collect payments from clients, assign work to employees and contractors, track building permits, manage factory orders, calculate sales commissions, and monitor labor and material charges.
