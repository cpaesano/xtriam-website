# Customizing Sales Document Clauses in bpmPro

Sales document clauses are the terms and conditions that appear at the bottom of your proposals and contracts. bpmPro gives you full control over these clauses — you can edit the text, format it with rich text, reorder clauses, hide them temporarily, control which document types they appear on, and create custom section headers.

---

## Step 1 — Navigate to Sales Document Clauses

From the homepage, go to the **Command Center** and scroll down to the **Dealer Management** section. Click **Sales Document Clauses** to open the module.

If you don't see any clauses listed, your list view may be set to **Recently Viewed**. Switch the filter to **All** to display every clause. You can pin this view by clicking the **pin icon** so it loads by default next time.

---

## Step 2 — Understand the Clause Columns

The clauses list shows several columns:

| Column | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| **Clause Record Number** | Click to open and edit the clause |
| **Index** | Determines the order the clause appears in the sales document |
| **Title** | The clause heading |
| **Clause Text** | The actual terms and conditions content |

---

## Editing a Clause

Click the **pencil icon** next to a clause to edit it. The clause text field is a **rich text editor**, allowing you to:

- **Bold** text for emphasis
- Change **font size**
- **Underline** important terms
- Format content as needed

Click **Save** when done. To preview changes, open a sales document in another browser tab (right-click the tab and select **Duplicate** for easy switching), generate the presentation, and scroll to the **Terms and Conditions** section. Refresh the preview to see your updates immediately.

> **Tip:** Duplicating your browser tab lets you edit clause settings in one tab and preview the sales document in another — no need to navigate back and forth.

---

## Hiding a Clause

To temporarily hide a clause from all sales documents:

1. Open the clause record
2. Uncheck the **Active** checkbox
3. Click **Save**

The clause will no longer appear in any sales document. You can reactivate it at any time by checking the Active box again.

---

## Conditional Visibility by Document Type

You can control which clauses appear based on the document type. Each clause has checkboxes for:

- **Proposal**
- **Contract**
- **Service Contract**
- **Change Order**

For example, if a clause should only appear on proposals, open the clause and uncheck the boxes for Contract, Service Contract, and Change Order. Save your changes, and that clause will only be visible when viewing a proposal.

---

## Reordering Clauses and Adding Section Headers

Clauses are ordered by their **Index** value. To reorganize:

1. Update the index of existing clauses (e.g., change **3** to **3.1**, and the next clause to **3.2**)
2. Click **New** to create a header clause with index **3.0**
3. Enter a descriptive title and format it as bold with a larger font size in the rich text editor
4. Click **Save**

Refresh the sales document preview and you'll see your new section header above the reorganized clauses, helping visually separate different sections of your terms and conditions.

---

## Quick Reference

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| **Edit clause text** | Click the pencil icon → use the rich text editor → Save |
| **Hide a clause** | Open the clause → uncheck **Active** → Save |
| **Show only on proposals** | Open the clause → uncheck Contract, Service Contract, Change Order → Save |
| **Reorder clauses** | Change the **Index** values (e.g., 3.0, 3.1, 3.2) |
| **Add a section header** | Create a new clause with a .0 index and bold, large title text |
| **Preview changes** | Duplicate the browser tab → refresh the sales document preview |
