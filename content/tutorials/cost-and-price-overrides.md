# Cost and Price Overrides in bpmPro

This tutorial demonstrates how to apply cost and price overrides inside bpmPro, giving you full control over your quoting and pricing process. You'll learn how to adjust markup and cost dynamically, create manual overrides, reset items to auto-calculate, and apply bulk updates efficiently across multiple line items.

- How to exclude labor from a full-service quote using the **Exclude Labor** checkbox

- How to create a **manual price override** using the wrench tool
- What the **lock icon** means and how it indicates manually overridden pricing
- How to override the **product cost** and observe automatic margin adjustments
- How to **reset a line item** back to auto-calculation mode with one click

- How locked items are **protected and excluded** from mass updates

## Key Concepts

### Automatic Pricing

By default, bpmPro calculates prices automatically based on your markup percentage and product cost. When you change the markup, the price updates. When you change the cost, the margin adjusts. This keeps your numbers consistent without manual math.

### Manual Overrides (Wrench Tool)

When you need to set a specific price that doesn't follow the standard markup formula, use the **wrench tool** to create a manual override. This lets you enter an exact price for a line item.

### Lock Icon

Once a line item has a manual override, bpmPro displays a **lock icon** next to it. This tells you at a glance which items have custom pricing — and more importantly, locked items are **protected from mass updates** so your manual prices don't get accidentally overwritten.

### Resetting to Auto-Calculate

If you've overridden a price but want to go back to formula-based pricing, you can reset the line item with one click. This removes the lock and returns the item to automatic calculation based on your markup settings.

### Mass Markup Updates

When you need to change the markup across all line items at once, bpmPro lets you apply a **bulk update**. This updates every unlocked item in one step — while leaving any locked (manually overridden) items untouched.

## Quick Reference

| Action | How |
|--------|-----|
| **Override a price** | Click the **wrench tool** on the line item and enter the custom price |
| **Identify overridden items** | Look for the **lock icon** next to the line item |
| **Reset to auto-calculate** | Click the reset button on the locked line item |
| **Exclude labor** | Check the **Exclude Labor** checkbox on the line item |
| **Mass update markup** | Enter the new markup percentage and apply to all unlocked items |

> **Tip:** Use a combination of automatic and manual pricing for maximum flexibility. Let bpmPro calculate most items automatically, and only override the ones that need special pricing.
