# Creating Accounts in bpmPro

Accounts are the foundation of your data in bpmPro. Every contact, project, and sales document is linked to an account. Before you can create a lead or a quote, you need an account.

---

## Account Types

Accounts are categorized into 8 **Business Internal Roles**:

- **Client** — Homeowners or end customers
- **Homeowner Association** — HOAs that may require approval
- **Labor Subcontractor** — Installation crews or subcontracted labor
- **Municipality** — Permit offices, building departments
- **Other** — Any account that doesn't fit the other categories
- **Owning Entity** — Your own company (the window dealer)
- **Product Vendor** — Manufacturers and suppliers (e.g., PGT, ES Windows)
- **Professional Services** — Engineers, architects, consultants

![Account record showing the Business Internal Role dropdown with all 8 options](/images/tutorials/accounts_business_roles.jpeg)

---

## Step 1 — Navigate to the Accounts Tab

From the bpmPro Home Page, click on the **Accounts** tab in the top navigation bar, then click **New Account**.

![Accounts list view with the New Account button highlighted](/images/tutorials/accounts_list_new_account.jpeg)

---

## Step 2 — Fill Out the New Account Form

The New Account form will open. Fill out as much information as possible.

**Required fields:**
- **Account Name** — The customer or company name
- **Business Internal Role** — Select the appropriate role from the dropdown
- **Billing Email Address** — Primary email for the account

![New Account form showing required fields: Account Name, Business Internal Role, and Billing Email Address](/images/tutorials/accounts_new_account_form.jpeg)

---

## Step 3 — Select the Business Internal Role

Choose the appropriate role for the account you are creating. For example, if you are adding a window manufacturer like PGT or ES Windows, select **Product Vendor**.

![Business Internal Role dropdown with Product Vendor selected](/images/tutorials/accounts_product_vendor_role.jpeg)

Click **Save** when you are done.
