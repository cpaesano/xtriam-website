# Creating Contacts in bpmPro

Every account can have one or more contacts — homeowners, company employees, vendor reps, etc. Contacts are how you track the people associated with each account.

---

## Step 1 — Find the Account

Before creating a contact, navigate to the account where the contact belongs. Type the account name in the **global Search box** at the top of the screen.

![Global search showing results for John Smith with account and contact matches](/images/tutorials/contacts_global_search.jpeg)

Click the **Account** result (not the Contact) to open the account record.

---

## Step 2 — Open the Contacts Tab

Once inside the account record, click on the **Contacts** tab in the Account Workspace. This shows all contacts currently linked to the account.

![Account record for John Smith with the Contacts tab highlighted](/images/tutorials/contacts_account_contacts_tab.jpeg)

---

## Step 3 — Click New

In the Contacts section, click the **New** button to create a new contact under this account.

![Contacts list showing the New button to add a contact](/images/tutorials/contacts_list_new_button.jpeg)

---

## Step 4 — Fill Out the Contact Form

Fill out as much information as possible. The account name will be pre-filled since you are creating the contact from within the account.

**Key fields:**
- **First Name** and **Last Name** (required)
- **Category** — Select the contact type (e.g., Property Owner)
- **Mobile** and **Email** — Enter only if different from the account details

![New Contact form showing parent account, name fields, and contact category](/images/tutorials/contacts_new_contact_form.jpeg)

---

## Step 5 — Set Up Employee Contacts (Important)

If you are creating a contact for an **employee** of your company (a sales person, project manager, etc.), you must configure the **Employee Contact Internal Setup** section at the bottom of the form.

**Two things are required:**

1. **Internal Company Role** — Select the employee's role (e.g., Sales and Estimating, Operations, Management)
2. **Company Role Active** — Check this box to make the employee active in the system

> **Why this matters:** If you don't set the Internal Company Role and mark it as active, the employee will not appear in dropdown menus throughout bpmPro — such as when assigning a Sales Person to a quote or a Project Manager to a project.

![Employee Contact Internal Setup showing the Internal Company Role dropdown with roles like Sales and Estimating highlighted](/images/tutorials/contacts_internal_company_role.jpeg)

Click **Save** when you are done.

---

## Quick Reference

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | Search for the account using the global Search box |
| 2 | Open the account → click the **Contacts** tab |
| 3 | Click **New** |
| 4 | Fill out name, category, phone, and email |
| 5 | For employees: set **Internal Company Role** and check **Company Role Active** |
| 6 | Click **Save** |
