Timesheets and Time Entries

Overview:
The Timesheet system tracks employee hours worked on a weekly basis. Employees enter their daily clock-in/clock-out times, and supervisors can review and approve timesheets. The system supports payroll export and overtime calculations.

How to Access:
From the Command Center, click the "Timesheet Console" tile. You can also access Timesheets and Time Entries from their respective tabs.

How Timesheets Work:

Weekly Structure:
- Each timesheet covers one week (Monday through Sunday)
- Employees create one timesheet per week
- Time entries are the individual daily records within a timesheet

Creating Time Entries:
1. Open or create a timesheet for the current week
2. For each day worked, add a time entry
3. Enter the clock-in time and clock-out time
4. Select the activity type (Installation, Service, Admin, Travel, etc.)
5. Optionally link to a Project
6. Add break time in minutes
7. Hours are calculated automatically from clock-in/out minus break time

Activity Types:
There are over 40 activity types to categorize work, including: Installation, Service and Maintenance, Admin, Travel, Inspection, Fabrication, Repair, Estimating and Quoting, Permit Processing, Warehouse, and many more.

Timesheet Status Flow:
- Draft: Employee is entering time, can edit freely
- Submitted: Employee has submitted for review, no further edits
- Approved: Supervisor has approved the timesheet
- Returned for Edits: Supervisor sent it back with notes, employee can edit again

Submitting a Timesheet:
1. Make sure all days are filled in
2. Review the weekly total hours
3. Click "Submit for Review"
4. Once submitted, you cannot edit until a supervisor returns it

Supervisor Approval:
1. Open the Team Timesheets view
2. Review each submitted timesheet
3. Click "Approve" or "Return" with an optional note
4. Returned timesheets go back to Draft for the employee to fix

Common Issues:

Cannot edit my timesheet:
- Your timesheet may be in Submitted status
- Ask your supervisor to return it if changes are needed

Hours showing as zero:
- Make sure both clock-in and clock-out times are entered
- Hours are calculated automatically from the time difference

Missing a day:
- You can add time entries for any day within the timesheet week
- Each day can have multiple entries (e.g., morning on one project, afternoon on another)

Tips:
- Enter time daily rather than waiting until the end of the week
- Use the correct activity type so payroll and project costing are accurate
- Link time entries to projects when applicable for job costing reports
- Break time is subtracted from the total hours automatically
