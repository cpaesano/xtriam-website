Sales Document Accounting (Deal Sheet)

Overview:
The Sales Document Accounting Console provides the financial management view for a Sales Document. It shows the deal sheet with cost breakdowns, tax calculations, and allows creation of chargebacks for vendor adjustments.

How to Access:
Open a Sales Document record page. The Accounting Console appears as a component on the page, typically visible to managers and accounting users.

Key Features:

Deal Sheet:
The deal sheet shows the complete financial breakdown:
- Product costs and markups
- Labor costs
- Professional services
- Materials
- Tax breakdown (state and county, wholesale and retail)
- Grand total with all fees

Tax Breakdown:
- State sales tax and county tax are calculated separately
- Wholesale tax and retail tax are shown individually
- Total tax line combines all components

Chargebacks:
Use chargebacks to track vendor-related cost adjustments:
1. Click "Add Chargeback"
2. Select the category
3. Enter the amount, quantity, and vendor
4. Add a description
5. Chargebacks are tracked separately from customer-facing invoices

Accounting Package PDF:
The accounting package can be exported as a PDF for record-keeping. It includes all financial details, cost breakdowns, and tax calculations.

Common Issues:

Tax amounts seem wrong:
- Verify the tax rates in Company Settings
- Check if county tax is configured correctly
- New tax rates only apply to new invoices

Cannot see the accounting section:
- This section may be restricted to management and accounting roles
- Check your permissions with your admin

Tips:
- Review the accounting console before closing a deal to verify all costs
- Use chargebacks for vendor issues rather than modifying line item costs
- The accounting package is separate from the customer-facing proposal PDF
