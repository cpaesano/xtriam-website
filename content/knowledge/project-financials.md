Project Financials Dashboard

Overview:
The Project Financials Dashboard gives you a complete financial picture of a project. It shows contracted amounts, invoiced totals, payments received, and cost breakdowns so you can track profitability in real time.

How to Access:
Open any Project record page. The Financial Summary component appears in the sidebar or detail section.

What It Shows:

Contract Summary:
- Contracted Amount: Total value of all sold Sales Documents
- Invoiced Amount: How much has been billed to the customer
- Unbilled Amount: Remaining amount not yet invoiced
- Total Payments: How much the customer has paid
- Balance Due: Outstanding amount still owed

Cost Breakdown:
The dashboard separates costs by category:
- Product Cost: Materials and products from vendors
- Labor Cost: Installation and service labor
- Pro Services: Professional services (engineering, permits processing)
- Materials: Additional materials not tied to specific products
- Permit Fees: Government permit costs

Profitability:
- Estimated vs Actual costs for each category
- Variance showing over/under budget
- Gross Profit calculation
- Margin percentages

Key Indicators:
- Green: On or under budget
- Red: Over budget
- The dashboard auto-refreshes when invoice or payment data changes

Common Issues:

Financials not matching expectations:
- Verify all Sales Documents are in the correct status
- Check that permit fees are properly categorized
- Cost entries on line items must be populated for accurate margin calculations

Profit showing as zero:
- Cost fields on A3 line items may not be filled in
- Check the dealer cost and labor cost on each line item

Tips:
- Review financials before closing a deal to verify margins
- Use this dashboard during project reviews to identify cost overruns early
- Permit handling costs and actual permit fees are separated so they do not distort product margins
