Permit Fees

Overview:
Permit Fees track the costs of building permits for projects. You can mark fees as billable to the customer and include them on invoices. The system separates permit handling fees (what you charge the customer) from actual permit costs (what the municipality charges).

How to Access:
Open a Project Invoice record. The Permit Fee section appears in the Invoice Payment Console.

Managing Permit Fees:

Adding Permit Fees:
1. Open the Project Invoice Payment Console
2. Navigate to the Permit Fees section
3. Click "Add Permit Fee"
4. Enter the fee amount and description
5. Mark as billable if the customer should be charged

Billable vs Non-Billable:
- Billable fees are included in the invoice total and shown on the customer-facing PDF
- Non-billable fees are tracked for internal cost reporting only
- Toggle the billable status on each permit fee

Invoicing Permit Fees:
- Billable permit fees appear as line items on the project invoice
- The summary shows: total billable, total invoiced, and total uninvoiced
- Permit fee payments are tracked separately from product/labor payments

Impact on Financials:
- Permit handling costs and actual permit fees are separated in the Project Financials Dashboard
- This prevents permit costs from distorting your product and labor profit margins

Common Issues:

Permit fee not showing on invoice:
- Check that the fee is marked as "Billable"
- Verify it is linked to the correct Project Invoice

Total paid seems wrong:
- Permit fee payments are stored separately from Sales Document Invoice payments
- The Project Invoice total includes both SDI payments and permit fee payments

Tips:
- Always mark customer-billable permit fees as billable so they appear on invoices
- Use the uninvoiced amount to see which permit fees still need to be billed
- The financials dashboard separates permit costs for accurate profit reporting
