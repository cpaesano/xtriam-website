E-Signature

Overview:
The E-Signature feature allows you to send Sales Documents to customers for electronic signature. The customer receives a link, reviews the document, and signs it on their phone or computer. Once signed, the document status updates automatically and the sales rep is notified.

How to Access:
Open any Sales Document, then use the "Send for E-Sign" option from the Send Document wizard or the quick action menu.

How It Works:

Sending for Signature:
1. Open the Sales Document you want signed
2. Click "Send Document" or the e-sign action
3. Select the recipient (customer email)
4. The customer receives an email with a link to view and sign the document
5. The link opens a secure web page showing the document details and a signature pad

Customer Experience:
1. Customer clicks the link in their email
2. They see the full document with line items, pricing, and payment schedule
3. They draw their signature on the signature pad
4. They click "Accept and Sign"
5. A confirmation screen shows the document is signed

After Signing:
- The Sales Document status changes to "Accepted"
- The sales rep receives a notification email
- The signed date and signer information are recorded
- The document is locked from further edits
- A signed banner appears on the document showing who signed and when

Document Status Flow:
Unquoted, Being Quoted, Quoted, then Sent for Signature, Accepted (after customer signs)

Common Issues:

Customer says link is not working:
- Check that the email was sent to the correct address
- The link is valid for a limited time
- Ask the customer to check their spam folder

Document not showing as signed:
- The system polls for signature status periodically
- It may take a few minutes to update
- Try refreshing the Sales Document record page

Customer wants to make changes after signing:
- Signed documents are locked to protect the agreement
- To make changes, create a new version using Clone Plus and send that for signature

Tips:
- Always verify the customer email address before sending
- The customer can sign on any device including phones, tablets, and computers
- The grand total and payment schedule are clearly displayed to the customer before they sign
- Once a document is accepted, it cannot be modified
