Industry Challenges

We Understand What Makes This Business Hard

The window and door industry has challenges that generic CRM software doesn't account for. We know them because we've dealt with every single one.

Impact Window Regulations

Florida's building codes require impact-rated products in wind-borne debris regions. Every project involves product approvals, NOA (Notice of Acceptance) numbers, and county-specific permit requirements. Your CRM needs to handle this complexity — not ignore it.

Complex Quoting

A single window project can involve dozens of product configurations — frame types, glass options, sizes, hardware, screens, colors. Add county tax rates, labor by opening type, and manufacturer price tiers, and you have a quoting process that spreadsheets can't handle reliably.

Price Volatility

Aluminum, glass, and hardware prices fluctuate constantly. Manufacturers issue price increases with 30–60 days' notice. If your quoting system doesn't reflect current costs, you're giving away margin on every job. We've seen contractors lose 3–5% per project from outdated pricing alone.

Supply Chain Coordination

Window and door projects depend on manufacturers, distributors, and delivery schedules you don't fully control. Lead times range from 2 to 12 weeks depending on the product. If your scheduling system doesn't track material status, your crews show up to jobs that aren't ready.

Long Project Cycles

From initial quote to final payment, a window replacement project can take 3–6 months. That means managing deposits, progress payments, customer expectations, and project milestones across dozens of active jobs simultaneously. Without a system, things fall through the cracks.

Customer Communication Gap

The number one complaint homeowners have during a window project: “Nobody tells me what's going on.” And the number one time-waster for your office: answering “Where is my order?” calls. Both problems exist because communication depends on someone remembering to send an update.
